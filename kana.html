◌→◌゙ ◌→◌゚ voices the consonant.
「い」「う」 and sometimes 「お」 vowels may be devoiced at the end of words or between unvoiced consonants.
As a particle, 「は」 sounds like 「わ」.
As a particle, 「を」 sounds like 「お」.
As a particle, 「へ」 sounds like 「え」.

A vowel is lengthened by adding an 「あ」「い」「う」 or 「ー」.
A consonant is doubled by adding a small 「っ」「ッ」.
「〜ん」 may become more nasal depending on the following consonant.
